persuasion principles

these are a collection of principles we learned throughout the semester in persuasion.

my goal was to create a visual system that could give information on how the principle is perceived by means of sentiment analysis and in terms of its utilization of positive/negative emotions, and conscious/subconscious methods of persuasion.

the sentiment analysis is used as an objective source to determine how positive or negative the term is. as a result, some terms that aren't more commonly used have a neutral color. the sentiment analysis score (from -1 to 1) is on the bottom right of the card. the colors indicate a positive, neutral, or negative score.

the gradient on the front of the card is used to visualize more subjective labels on two axes (example on the right) the positive/negative emotions commonly associated with the term, and the use of conscious/subconscious methods.

on the back of the cards (click to flip!), i write the definition, as well as my reasoning for the subjective labels.
